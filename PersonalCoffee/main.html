The rostery.

Roasting brings out the aroma and flavour that is locked inside the green coffee beans which are properly stored. This means they are in a state in which they can be kept without loss of quality or taste. A green bean has none of the characteristics of a roasted bean; it is soft and spongy to the bite with a grassy smell.


Roasting causes chemical changes to take place as the beans are rapidly brought to very high temperatures. When they reach the peak of perfection, they are quickly cooled to stop the process. Roasted beans smell like coffee, and weigh less because the moisture has been roasted out. They are crunchy to the bite, ready to be ground and brewed.

Roast types.

The perfect roast is a personal choice, for that we let you chose the best one for you!


Light roast.

Light brown in color, this roast is generally preferred for milder coffee varieties. There will be no oil on the surface of these beans because they are not roasted long enough for the oils to break through to the surface.


Medium roast.

This roast is medium brown in color with a stronger flavor and a non-oily surface. It’s often referred to as the American roast


Medium dark roasts

Rich, dark color, this roast has some oil on the surface and with a slight bittersweet aftertaste.


Dark roasts

This roast produces shiny black beans with an oily surface and a pronounced bitterness. The darker the roast, the less acidity will be found in the coffee beverage.
